Scholarship across borders

Dr. Huping Ling is Professor Emerita of History at Truman State University and a leading scholar of Asian American history. Her work examines Chinese American communities, transnational migration, women and family, and the changing geography of Asian America.

She is the recipient of the 2024 Lifetime Achievement Award from the Association for Asian American Studies and has authored or edited 37 books and more than 200 articles.

Her scholarship connects archival research with personal stories, oral history, and public engagement—bringing overlooked communities in the American heartland into broader histories of migration, race, and belonging.

Current & former appointments

  • Professor Emerita of History, Truman State University
  • Former Visiting Research Fellow, Hoover Institution, Stanford University
  • Changjiang Scholar Chair Professor, Chinese Ministry of Education
  • Distinguished Honorary Professor, Lishui University
  • Visiting Professor, Institute of Overseas Chinese Studies, Jinan University
  • Founding editor, Asian American Studies Today, Rutgers University Press
